Absolutely! Bertie Wooster is a linguistic goldmine. Here are some favorites:

**BERTIE'S VERBS FOR MENTAL/EMOTIONAL STATES:**

- **Biffed** - hit, struck (also emotionally)
- **Pipped** - defeated, beaten
- **Snookered** - trapped, in difficulty
- **Stymied** - thwarted, blocked
- **Dished** - ruined, done for
- **Sunk** - defeated, without hope
- **Pushed off** / **toddled off** - left, departed
- **Buzzed off** - left quickly
- **Beetled off** - hurried away
- **Legged it** - ran away
- **Oiled off** - slipped away
- **Shimmied** - moved/danced
- **Navigated** - moved (often drunkenly)

**STATES OF BEING:**

- **In the soup** - in trouble
- **In the cart** - in difficulty
- **Up a gum tree** - stuck, in difficulty
- **Behind the eight ball** - in trouble
- **Put the bee on** - hit up for money
- **Touch someone for** - ask to borrow money
- **A bit of a lad** - fun-loving fellow
- **A good egg** - decent person
- **A bad egg** / **tough egg** / **hard-boiled egg** - unpleasant person
- **A pip** - something/someone excellent
- **The goods** - the real thing, excellent

**INTELLIGENCE & MENTAL CAPACITY:**

- **Not a brain in his head** / **as much brain as a rabbit**
- **Loony** / **off his onion** - crazy
- **Cracked** / **potty** / **dotty** - crazy
- **Barmy** - insane
- **The old bean** - head, brain ("use the old bean")
- **Grey matter** - intelligence
- **Bright lad** - intelligent person (often ironic)

**EMOTIONAL/PHYSICAL STATES:**

- **All of a twitter** - nervous, excited
- **In a doodah** - agitated
- **In a flat spin** - panicked
- **Hot under the collar** - angry
- **Shirty** - bad-tempered
- **Browned off** - fed up
- **Fed to the tonsils** / **fed to the back teeth** - completely fed up
- **Peeved** - annoyed
- **Shaken to the core** / **rocked to his foundations**
- **Come apart at the seams**
- **Gone all to pieces**

**STUNTS & SCHEMES:**

- **A wheeze** - a scheme, plan, stunt (as you noted!)
- **A ruse** - trick
- **A caper** - escapade
- **A lark** - amusing scheme
- **The needle** - annoyance, irritation ("give someone the needle")

**FOOD & DRINK:**

- **Vitals** - food
- **The material** - food/drink
- **Bite of lunch** / **spot of lunch**
- **A snifter** - a drink
- **Bracer** - alcoholic drink (for courage)
- **Restorative** - drink
- **The juice of the grape** - wine
- **Oof** - money (schoolboy slang)

**ROMANTIC/SOCIAL:**

- **Smitten** - in love
- **Potty about** - infatuated with
- **Carrying a torch** - in love (unrequited)
- **Fancy someone** - be attracted to
- **Tangled up with** - romantically involved
- **Getting spliced** - getting married
- **Tying the knot** - marrying
- **Old crumpet** - woman (affectionate but dated)
- **Bit of fluff** - woman (not respectful)
- **The eternal feminine** - women generally

**APPEARANCE & IMPRESSION:**

- **Natty** - stylish, smart
- **Dapper** - well-dressed
- **Spruce** - neat, tidy
- **The pink of perfection** - perfectly dressed
- **Cut a dash** - make an impression
- **Look like nothing on earth** - look terrible
- **Presentable** - acceptable appearance

**APPROVAL/DISAPPROVAL:**

- **Rummy** / **rum** - odd, strange
- **Fruity** - rich, excessive (also risqu√©)
- **Juicy** - interesting, scandalous
- **Top-hole** / **top-notch** - excellent
- **Tipping** / **topping** / **ripping** - excellent
- **Sound** - good, reliable
- **Not on** - unacceptable
- **Not cricket** - unfair, not sporting
- **A bit thick** - unfair, too much
- **Beyond the pale** - unacceptable

**JEEVES-SPECIFIC LANGUAGE:**

- **Facial expression remaining wooden** - Jeeves maintaining impassivity
- **Faint shudder** - Jeeves's disapproval
- **Cough, soft and low** - Jeeves getting attention
- **Allow me to observe, sir** - Jeeves about to correct Bertie
- **I fear** - Jeeves disagrees
- **I would not recommend** - Jeeves forbids
- **The psychology of the individual** - Jeeves's analytical approach
- **I endeavor to give satisfaction** - Jeeves's modest claim

**BERTIE'S LITERARY ALLUSIONS:**

- **Quoting Shakespeare (badly)**
- **"As somebody-or-other once said"** - vague literary reference
- **Biblical references (mangled)**
- **"The bard"** - Shakespeare
- **"The poet"** - usually means someone classical

**BERTIE'S CHARACTERISTIC PHRASES:**

- **"What ho!"** - greeting
- **"I say!"** - exclamation
- **"Dash it!"** / **"Dash it all!"** - mild oath
- **"By Jove!"** - exclamation
- **"Right ho!"** - agreement
- **"Rather!"** - enthusiastic yes
- **"Absolutely!"**
- **"What?"** - asking for repetition/emphasis
- **"Don't you know"** - verbal filler
- **"I mean to say"** - verbal filler
- **"As it were"** - qualifier

**PHYSICAL VIOLENCE (always comic):**

- **Sock someone** - hit
- **Biff** - hit
- **Plug** - hit
- **Slosh** - hit
- **Dot in the eye** - punch
- **Knock spots off** - beat thoroughly
- **Take a pop at** - try to hit

**BERTIE'S METAPHORS:**

- **"Like a warrior taking a short breather before plunging into the fray"**
- **"As absent as a drum with a hole in it"**
- **"As touchy as a tigress deprived of her cubs"**
- **"Stiff upper lip and what not"**
- **"Keep the flag flying"**
- **"Bite the bullet"**

The genius of Wodehouse is that Bertie's slang is both authentically upper-class 1920s-30s AND completely distinctive to his character - he mangles quotes, mixes metaphors, and creates delightful verbal chaos while somehow remaining perfectly comprehensible!
