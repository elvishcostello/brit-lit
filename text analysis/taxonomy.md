# **COMPREHENSIVE TAXONOMY OF BRITISH FICTION TERMS**

## **I. SOCIAL STRUCTURE & CLASS**

### **A. Titles & Forms of Address**

- **Duke/Duchess of Denver** - highest peerage rank (Sayers)
- **Lord Peter Wimsey** - courtesy title for duke's younger son (Sayers)
- **Lady Mary Wimsey** - courtesy title for duke's daughter (Sayers)
- **your grace** - formal address for duke/duchess (Sayers)
- **Hon. Frederick Arbuthnot** - "Honourable," for children of viscounts/barons (Sayers)
- **Honourable Mrs Jamieson** - courtesy title (wife/daughter of peer) (Cranford)
- **Sir William/Lady Lucas** - knighthood title (Austen)
- **late Earl of Glenmire** - deceased earl (Cranford)
- **Major/Captain** - military ranks/titles (Cranford)
- **Master Davy** - form of address for young boy (Dickens)

### **B. Social Class & Status Indicators**

- **gentlefolks** - people of gentle (upper-class) birth (Cranford)
- **genteel society** - respectable upper-class society (Cranford)
- **aristocratic** - of aristocratic class/bearing (Cranford)

- **good breeding** - good manners/upbringing (Austen)
- **well bred** - well-mannered (Austen)
- **air of decided fashion** - fashionable appearance (Austen)
- **supercilious** - arrogant/disdainful (Austen)
- **fastidious** - hard to please (Austen)
- **people of rank** - aristocracy/upper class (Austen)
- **man of fashion** - fashionable/stylish man (Austen)
- **shopkeeper's niece** - major social shame (Cranford)
- **in trade** - engaged in commerce (lower class) (Austen)
- **acquired by trade** - earned through business vs. inherited (Austen)
- **the poor** - lower classes (Cranford)

### **C. Social Behaviors & Judgments**

- **sent to Coventry** - socially ostracized (Cranford)
- **the Spartans** - comparison to Spartans (hiding hardship) (Cranford)
