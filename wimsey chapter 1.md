
## **Titles & Forms of Address**

- **Duke/Duchess of Denver** - British peerage title
- **Lord Peter Wimsey** - courtesy title for duke's younger son
- **Lady Mary Wimsey** - courtesy title for duke's daughter
- **your grace** - formal address for duke/duchess
- **Hon. Frederick Arbuthnot** - "Honourable," title for children of viscounts/barons

## **Institutions & Settings**

- **the House** - Christ Church, Oxford (very British to refer to Oxford colleges this way)
- **shooting-box** - small lodge/cottage for hunting parties
- **conservatory** - glass-enclosed room/greenhouse attached to house
- **gun-room** - room for storing hunting weapons
- **the moors** - moorland for grouse shooting
- **bowling-green** - lawn for lawn bowling
- **Scotland Yard** - London police headquarters

## **Occupations**

- **gamekeeper** - estate worker who manages game/prevents poaching
- **manservant** - male domestic servant (Bunter)

## **Legal/Procedural**

- **inquest** - coroner's inquiry (distinctively British legal system)
- **in the dock** - defendant's place in British courtroom
- **in the witness-box** - witness stand

## **Slang & Idioms**

- **deuced** - very/extremely ("deuced awkward," "deuced queer")
- **don't you know** - upper-class verbal filler
- **toddled** - walked casually ("toddled on up")
- **having a last peg** - having a final drink (whisky and soda)
- **turned in** - gone to bed
- **hell-for-leather** - at great speed
- **dust-up** - argument/quarrel
- **getting across [someone]** - annoying/quarreling with
- **hard up** - short of money
- **chaw his head off** - scold severely
- **buttin' in** - interfering
- **had a row** - had an argument
- **knocked me up** - woke me up (⚠️ very different American meaning!)
- **uncommonly** - very/extremely
- **plucky** - brave
- **Sahib** - gentleman (from colonial India)
- **right oh!** - all right/okay

## **Clothing**

- **dinner-jacket** - tuxedo (American: "tux")
- **pumps** - formal men's shoes
- **covert-coat** - type of outdoor overcoat

## **Objects**

- **electric torch** - flashlight
- **sal volatile** - smelling salts

## **Other Expressions**

- **the post came** - mail arrived
- **half-past nine** - 9:30 (British time-telling style)
- **quarter past ten** - 10:15
- **took it on myself** - took the liberty

## **Social Context Terms**

- **my man** - my valet/servant
- **country house party** - the entire social setting
- **poachers** - people illegally hunting on private land
